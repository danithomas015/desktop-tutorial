Atari CX2600 VCS

Atari CX2600 VCS was a highly successful home entertainment system that set the trend for future developments in the field. It was designed to counter the rapid redundancy experienced by dedicated console products like Pong by creating a flexible system that accepted carts with program information for a game. Sales were initially poor, but were greatly boosted when Atari licensed exclusive rights to produce a home version of the popular video arcade game "Space Invaders". The Atari 2600 console was discontinued in 1991, but its continued appeal may be attributed to several factors including the compelling nature of some of the games, the "vintage" nature of the graphic style and the ability to play "classic" arcade games..

Magnavox Odyssey

The Magnavox Odyssey, designed by Ralph Baer in 1967-68, was the first ever video game system designed for televisions in the home and initiated the home TV game market. It was first demonstrated to the public in early 1972 and released for commercial sale in August of 1972, pre-dating Atari's Pong console by at least three years. There were several iterations of the Odyssey console, including Pong versions and Odyssey2.

Nintendo Entertainment System (NES)

he Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) revolutionized video gaming in the mid-late 1980s by reestablishing Nintendo with a new business model for third-party developers after a crash in the video game market from 1983-85. The NES hit the market in 1986 with certain modifications to its predecessor, the Famicon, and was packaged in several different ways. The NES control decks were discontinued in 1995.

Intellivision Home System

The Intellivision was a home video game console released by Mattel Electronics in 1979. It was designed to compete with the Atari 2600 and had advanced features such as better graphics and sound. The Intellivision had a unique controller with a numeric keypad and side buttons, which allowed for more complex gameplay. The console had a library of games, including popular titles like "Astrosmash" and "Utopia." The Intellivision was discontinued in 1990, but its legacy can still be seen in modern gaming, with its innovative controller design inspiring future controllers such as the Nintendo Switch Joy-Cons.
